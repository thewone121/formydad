Privacy Policy

How we collect, use and protect your personal information.

Last updated: 2026. Template — please have this reviewed by the firm before publication.

Pentium Chambers ("the firm", "we", "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of those who visit our website and engage our services. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices available to you.

Information we collect

  • Information you provide. When you complete a contact, consultation, career or newsletter form, we collect the details you submit — such as your name, organisation, email, telephone number and the content of your message.
  • Technical information. Like most websites, we may collect limited technical data (such as browser type and pages visited) to operate and improve the site.

How we use your information

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide the legal services you request.
  • To consider applications for employment or internships.
  • To send occasional updates where you have asked us to.
  • To comply with our legal and regulatory obligations.

Lawful basis

We process personal data on the basis of your consent, the performance of a contract, our legitimate interests in operating the firm, and compliance with applicable law, including Nigeria's data-protection framework.

Sharing your information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share it with trusted service providers who support our operations, or where required by law, regulation or court order — always subject to appropriate safeguards.

Data security & retention

We take reasonable measures to protect personal information against loss, misuse and unauthorised access. We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy or as required by law.

Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data, and you may withdraw consent where processing is based on it. To exercise these rights, contact us using the details below.

Cookies

We use minimal cookies necessary to operate the site and to understand how it is used. You can control cookies through your browser settings and the on-site cookie notice.
