Corporate Governance in Nigeria: The Board's Duty of Care

As regulators and investors raise their expectations, Nigerian directors face a sharper duty of care than ever before. We map the modern governance framework — and the practical steps boards can take to meet it.

CORPORATE ADVISORY · 8 MIN READ

Corporate governance in Nigeria has matured from a box-ticking exercise into a genuine measure of a company's resilience. Directors owe fiduciary duties of care, skill and loyalty, and are increasingly held to account for how — not merely whether — decisions are taken.

What the modern standard expects

  • Informed decision-making supported by proper board papers and minutes.
  • Clear separation of oversight and management, with functioning committees.
  • Robust conflict-of-interest and related-party-transaction controls.
  • Transparent disclosure to shareholders and regulators.

Boards that treat governance as a strategic asset — rather than a compliance cost — tend to attract better capital, weather disputes more effectively, and preserve value through transition.

Good governance is not the absence of risk; it is the presence of discipline.

LITIGATION · 6 MIN READ

The most valuable commercial-litigation work often happens long before a writ is filed. Contract drafting, record-keeping and early strategic assessment frequently determine the outcome of a dispute more than courtroom advocacy alone.

Three habits that strengthen your position

  • Draft dispute-resolution and jurisdiction clauses deliberately, not by rote.
  • Preserve a clean, contemporaneous documentary record.
  • Assess the merits — candidly — before committing to litigation.

Where a claim must be pursued, disciplined preparation and a clear theory of the case are what turn strong facts into favourable judgments.

TECHNOLOGY LAW · 10 MIN READ

Artificial intelligence is arriving in Nigerian commerce faster than dedicated legislation can follow. In the interim, businesses must navigate AI through existing frameworks — data protection, contract, intellectual property, consumer protection and sector regulation.

Questions every AI-adopting business should ask

  • Who is liable when an automated decision causes harm?
  • What personal data trains the model, and on what lawful basis?
  • Who owns the outputs — and the inputs?
  • How are decisions explained to customers and regulators?

A thoughtful governance posture today will spare businesses difficult retrofits as Nigeria's AI regulation takes shape.

EMPLOYMENT · 6 MIN READ

The employment contract is the single most important instrument in the employer–employee relationship. Clear terms on role, remuneration, confidentiality, termination and dispute resolution prevent the majority of workplace disputes.

  • Define termination rights and notice periods precisely.
  • Protect confidential information and legitimate business interests.
  • Align policies with statutory obligations and best practice.

Well-drafted contracts and handbooks are far cheaper than the disputes they avoid.

CORPORATE · 7 MIN READ

Incorporating a company at the Corporate Affairs Commission is straightforward in principle — but the choices made at formation shape a business for years. Structure, share capital and constitutional documents deserve real thought.

Key decisions at incorporation

  • Choose the right vehicle for liability, tax and governance.
  • Set share structure and ownership deliberately.
  • Tailor the articles to how the business will actually run.
  • Put founder and shareholder arrangements in writing early.

TAX · 6 MIN READ

Tax compliance is a continuous discipline, not an annual scramble. Businesses that build compliance into their operations reduce exposure to penalties, interest and reputational risk.

  • Register for the correct taxes and maintain accurate records.
  • Meet filing and remittance deadlines consistently.
  • Understand withholding obligations on payments.
  • Seek advice before entering complex or cross-border transactions.

DATA PROTECTION · 7 MIN READ

Any organisation that handles personal data in Nigeria now operates under a defined statutory regime. Compliance is both a legal obligation and a marker of trust with customers.

First steps to compliance

  • Map the personal data you collect, why, and where it flows.
  • Establish a lawful basis for each processing activity.
  • Implement security safeguards and breach-response plans.
  • Honour data-subject rights and maintain records of processing.

Early, structured compliance is far less costly than remediation after an incident.

PRACTICE · 5 MIN READ

Precise drafting is where risk is quietly won or lost. A well-drafted agreement anticipates disputes and resolves them on paper before they ever reach a courtroom.

  • Say exactly what you mean — ambiguity favours the other side.
  • Define terms once and use them consistently.
  • Provide for the unexpected: default, delay and termination.

Good drafting is not ornamental; it is the architecture of the deal.

BANKING · 8 MIN READ

Nigeria's banking sector operates within a dense regulatory framework. For both lenders and borrowers, understanding how security is created, perfected and enforced is essential to protecting value.

  • Ensure security interests are properly created and perfected.
  • Understand receivership and enforcement pathways in advance.
  • Document facilities clearly to withstand later dispute.

Drawing on the firm's deep banking experience, we help both sides transact with confidence and enforce with precision.
