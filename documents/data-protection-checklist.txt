DATA PROTECTION — STARTER CHECKLIST
Pentium Chambers · General information only, not legal advice.
[PLACEHOLDER GUIDE — to be expanded and formatted by the firm.]

1.  Map the personal data you collect, why, and where it flows.
2.  Establish a lawful basis for each processing activity.
3.  Publish a clear, accurate privacy notice.
4.  Obtain and record valid consent where required.
5.  Implement technical & organisational security safeguards.
6.  Put a data-breach detection and response plan in place.
7.  Honour data-subject rights (access, correction, deletion).
8.  Maintain records of processing activities.
9.  Review third-party/processor contracts for data terms.
10. Assign responsibility for data protection within the organisation.
